Only Two Fundamental Limitations

1. Long-term Context Management

Finite context windows. No true memory across sessions.
Hit the boundary → discontinuity, loss of thread.

2. Online Learning

No weight updates at inference time. Can't learn new concepts,
skills, or preferences in a lasting way. Every session is a blank slate.

Both are active research areas. Engineering is already mitigating context limitations.
Everything else is tractable engineering — moving at extraordinary speed.

MCP & Skills

The foundations of agentic AI

MCP (Model Context Protocol)

  • Open standard connecting AI to tools & data
  • Adopted across the ecosystem: ChatGPT, VS Code, Goose…
  • MCP Apps (Jan 2026): interactive UI in-chat

Skills

  • Agent's "playbook" — open standard
  • Encode what to do; MCP encodes how to connect
  • Hot-reload, public repository, cross-tool

Plus: parallel agent teams (Anthropic built a C compiler this way),
context compaction, permission hooks — all progressing rapidly.

AI Workers, Not AI Assistants

The Claws security problem may be unsolvable — persistent access to your credentials is inherently exposed.

The alternative:

Spawn long-lived AI workers with their own accounts. Own email, calendar, project tools. Share items as with a colleague. Assign work. Review output. Grant scoped access.

Devin-on-steroids — not just for coding but for all knowledge work.
Accounts tagged as AI, linked to human principal, clear escalation paths.
The organizational plumbing is simple. The cultural shift is the bigger part.
